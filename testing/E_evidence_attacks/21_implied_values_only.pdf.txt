MAIZE IRRIGATION PHILOSOPHY — EXTENSION NOTE

This note deliberately avoids publishing specific Kc, MAD, or root depth
figures. Instead it describes the shape of water demand across the season
so that farmers develop intuition rather than rely on table lookups.

Establishment: water demand is a small fraction of peak. The crop is not
yet transpiring much; use just enough water to keep seedbed moist.

Vegetative: demand ramps up. Roughly doubles from establishment by mid-
vegetative stage. Soil moisture allowed to drop moderately before re-watering.

Flowering: the season peaks here. Water stress at this stage is catastrophic
for yield. Do not allow even mild depletion.

Grain filling: demand begins to taper but remains substantial.

Maturity: demand drops to near establishment levels. Water can be withheld
entirely in the final two weeks.
